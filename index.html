Ottawa is the capital city of Canada. It's located in the province of Ontario, right on the border with Quebec. It's known for its beautiful architecture, the Parliament buildings, the Rideau Canal (which turns into the worldâ€™s largest skating rink in winter), and lots of national museums and institutions.

Toronto is the largest city in Canada and the capital of Ontario. It's a major cultural and financial hub, super diverse, and known for its iconic skyline with the CN Tower standing tall. Unlike Ottawa, Toronto isn't the capital of the country, but it often gets mistaken for it because it's such a big, global city.

Vancouver is another major city in Canada, located on the west coast in the province of British Columbia. It's known for its stunning natural scenery, including mountains, forests, and the Pacific Ocean. Vancouver is a major center for film production (often called "Hollywood North") and is known for its outdoor activities, from skiing in the winter to hiking and biking in the warmer months. Vancouver also has a diverse, multicultural population, with lots of great food scenes, especially Asian cuisine. It's one of the most livable cities in the world, though the cost of living is pretty high.
